A quick overview of the HERB TRACE system.

1. Farmer Capture: The Source of Truth

Our journey begins at the farm. Farmers can easily log their harvest details using various methods, establishing the first immutable record of the herb's origin.

An Indian farmer harvesting turmeric and logging details via a mobile app, showing '5kg Turmeric' input.

2. Offline Support for Rural Farmers

Understanding the challenges of rural connectivity, HERB TRACE allows farmers to input data even without internet. Data is stored locally and automatically syncs when connectivity is restored.

A farmer in a rural field uses a smartphone to enter herb details, with an 'Offline - Sync Pending' notification.

3. AI-Powered Verification & Validation

To ensure authenticity and compliance, an advanced AI system automatically validates key details like species, geo-location, and seasonal restrictions against pre-defined rules. This minimizes human error and prevents fraudulent entries.

AI dashboard showing green check marks for location, season, and species recognition, with a farm map and seasonal calendar.

4. Secondary Collector & Two-Party Acknowledgement

For farmers without direct smartphone access, local collectors can assist by registering their harvest. Every handoff in the supply chain requires a two-party acknowledgment, creating an unbreakable chain of custody on the blockchain.

A local collector assists a farmer, using a smartphone to register herbs with a 'Verified on behalf of farmer' overlay.

5. Logistics & Quality Assurance

As herbs move from farm to processing, their journey is tracked. Lab technicians conduct quality tests, and reports are uploaded and verified on the system. Transport conditions and package integrity are also monitored.

A secure transport truck and a lab technician inspecting herbs, alongside a system interface showing lab report upload and transport details.

6. Geo-fencing & Smart Contract Enforcement

Smart contracts automatically enforce rules, such as geo-fencing for harvest locations. Any attempt to register herbs from an unapproved zone is immediately flagged and rejected, enhancing product integrity.

A digital map with a geo-fence, showing a farmer's phone receiving a 'Outside permitted zone' warning.

7. Blockchain & Batch Finalization

Once all checks are complete, batches are finalized on the blockchain, and unique QR codes are generated for each product. These codes serve as digital passports for the herbs.

A packaging factory printing QR codes on herbal product boxes, with a futuristic UI showing blockchain blocks and 'Batch ID Generated'.

8. Consumer Transparency: From Farm to Shelf

Consumers can simply scan the QR code on the product to access the herb's complete journey, building trust and confidence in their purchase.

A customer in a supermarket scanning a QR code, revealing the product's journey from farm to company on their phone.

9. Feedback Loop & Incentives

Consumer scans provide valuable insights for demand forecasting and help build trust. Verified farmers earn 'Green Premium' badges, recognizing their sustainable practices and potentially leading to better prices and demand.

A happy consumer scanning a product in a store, with data flowing back to a farmer receiving a 'Consumers trust your batch' notification.

10. Farmer Incentives: The Green Premium

Our system not only ensures transparency but also rewards farmers for sustainable and quality practices. Earning a "Green Premium Badge" provides recognition and market advantages.

An Indian farmer holding herbs, receiving a 'Green Premium Badge Earned' notification on their phone, indicating higher value.
